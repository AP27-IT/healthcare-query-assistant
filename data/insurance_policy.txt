Hospital Insurance Policy

1. Accepted Insurance Providers
The hospital accepts a range of insurance providers including Blue Cross,
Medicare, Aetna, UnitedHealthcare, and Cigna. Coverage details vary by plan,
and patients should verify their specific benefits before elective
procedures.

2. Pre-Authorization Requirements
Prior insurance approval, also known as pre-authorization, is required for
non-emergency surgeries, advanced imaging (MRI, CT scans), and extended
inpatient stays beyond the initial authorized period. Emergency procedures do
not require pre-authorization but must be reported to the insurer within 48
hours of admission.

3. Coverage Verification
The hospital's insurance desk verifies patient coverage at the time of
admission where possible. Patients are responsible for confirming that their
plan covers the specific hospital, physician, and treatment being provided,
as out-of-network care may result in higher out-of-pocket costs.

4. Denied Claims
If an insurance claim is denied, the billing department will notify the
patient in writing and provide guidance on the appeals process. Patients may
also file their own appeal directly with their insurance provider.

5. Uninsured Patients
Patients without insurance coverage are billed directly at standard rates and
may be eligible for the hospital's financial assistance program.
