Hospital Billing Policy

1. Billing Amount Composition
The total billing amount for a patient stay includes room charges, physician
fees, medication costs, laboratory and diagnostic test fees, and any
procedure-specific charges. Billing amounts are calculated per admission and
finalized at the time of discharge.

2. Payment Methods
The hospital accepts payment via insurance billing, credit or debit card,
bank transfer, and approved payment plans. Payment plans require prior
approval from the billing department and are available for balances exceeding
a set threshold.

3. Insurance Claims
For patients with insurance coverage, the hospital submits claims directly to
the listed insurance provider. Patients are responsible for any co-payment,
deductible, or amount not covered by their plan. Claim disputes must be
raised with the billing office within 60 days of the invoice date.

4. Billing Disputes
Patients who believe their bill contains an error may request an itemized
statement and file a formal dispute with the billing department within 30
days of receiving the invoice. Disputes are reviewed within 10 business days.

5. Financial Assistance
Patients experiencing financial hardship may apply for a reduction or waiver
of charges through the hospital's financial assistance program, subject to
income verification.
