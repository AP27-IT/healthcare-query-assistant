Hospital Discharge Policy

1. Discharge Criteria
A patient is eligible for discharge once the attending physician determines
that the patient's condition is stable and no longer requires inpatient
monitoring or treatment. Discharge decisions are documented in the patient's
medical record along with follow-up instructions.

2. Discharge Process
On the day of discharge, the care team provides the patient with a discharge
summary, prescription for any required medication, and instructions for
follow-up appointments. Nursing staff review post-discharge care instructions
with the patient or their caregiver before departure.

3. Against Medical Advice (AMA) Discharge
Patients who choose to leave before the physician recommends discharge must
sign an Against Medical Advice form acknowledging the risks. The hospital is
not liable for outcomes resulting from an AMA discharge.

4. Transfer to Another Facility
If a patient requires a level of care not available at the current facility,
the hospital will coordinate a transfer to an appropriate facility, including
arranging transport and forwarding medical records with patient consent.

5. Discharge Timing
Standard discharge time is by noon on the day of discharge. Late discharges
may incur an additional room charge unless approved by the attending
physician for medical reasons.
